Automagical setup

As soon as our snippet is loaded, Howdy will automatically handle your form

If you can copy and paste HTML you can add Howdy to your websites. Simply generate your snippet on this page and then copy & paste it to your page's HTML:

  • // Write your contact form just the way you like it:
  • <form>
  •   <input type=“text” name=“first_name”/>
  •   <input type=“text” name=“last_name”/>
  •   <textarea name=“message”></textarea>
  •   <input type=“submit”value=“Send message”/>
  • </form>
  • // Then paste in the snippet:
  • <script src=“…“ data-howdy-recipient=“your@email.com“></script>

You can even re-use the same snippet for different contact forms, just update the data-howdy-recipient attribute on the snippet to have it send to a different inbox:

  • <script src=“…“ data-howdy-recipient=“another@email.com“></script>

Professional form handling

Howdy automatically validates on all common fields & does custom validations too

Howdy will use the name attribute of inputs to determine how best to validate them:

  • email
  • name
  • first_name
  • last_name
  • firstName
  • lastName
  • subject
  • topic
  • about
  • body
  • message
  • address
  • postcode
  • post_code
  • postCode
  • zipcode
  • zip_code
  • zipCode
  • tel
  • telephone
  • mobile
  • mob
  • age
  • dob
  • date_of_birth
  • dateOfBirth

You can add data attributes to your inputs and textareas to add or override default validations:

  • data-howdy-validate=“none”// No validation on this input
  • data-howdy-validate=“required”// Some value is required
  • data-howdy-validate=“email”// Email address is required
  • data-howdy-validate=“number”// A number is required
  • data-howdy-validate=“date”// A date is required
  • data-howdy-validate=“tel”// No letters are allowed
